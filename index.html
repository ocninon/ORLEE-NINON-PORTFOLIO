UNIFIED CLASSIFICATION STANDARD (UCS)

The UCS is the single source of truth for classifying any software system, GPT, or AI tool by primary user outcome.

Knowledge, Documents & Research (KDR)

Outcome: Users obtain trusted understanding from text-based sources (internal or provided), or refine language with high fidelity.

Governance, Risk, Compliance & Legal (GRCL)

Outcome: Users enforce policy, manage risk, and produce audit-ready decision trails for regulated or controlled processes.

Data Acquisition & Offline Mirrors (DAOM)

Outcome: Users collect, mirror, and structure external web data for reuse elsewhere.

Analytics, Market & Operational Intelligence (AMOI)

Outcome: Users interpret numeric data to make decisions: forecasting, anomaly detection, alerting, and KPI-driven narrative.

Engineering Productivity, Automation & Toolchains (EPAT)

Outcome: Users build, run, and automate technical work: software delivery, integrations, operations, and execution.

Learning, Training & Assessment (LTA)

Outcome: Users teach, learn, and measure performance (progress, competency, certification).

Creative, Media & Experience Layers (CMEL)

Outcome: Users create or shape media/creative outputs or use high-fidelity presentation shells designed for showcasing modules and experiences.

Culture, Leisure & Faith (CLF)

Outcome: Users gain personal enrichment: spiritual well-being, heritage, entertainment, hobbies, and leisure planning.
